# How AI Room Redesign Works — The Technology Behind Vista

Ever wondered what happens between uploading a room photo and getting a photorealistic redesign? Here's the step-by-step process Vista uses to transform your space.

## Step 1: Room Analysis

When you upload a photo, Vista's AI analyzes the image to understand your room's structure:

- **Room dimensions** — approximate width, depth, and ceiling height
- **Architectural features** — walls, windows, doors, and their positions
- **Room type** — living room, bedroom, kitchen, etc.
- **Existing layout** — what's currently in the space

This structural understanding ensures the redesign respects your room's physical constraints.

## Step 2: Design Intent

You provide a text prompt describing what you want — a style, mood, or specific requests. Vista's AI interprets this alongside the room analysis to form a design brief: what furniture categories are needed, what style direction to take, and what materials and colors to use.

## Step 3: Catalog Matching

This is where Vista differs from generic AI image generators. Instead of inventing fictional furniture, Vista searches a real product catalog — sourced from Armenian marketplace retailers — and finds products that match the design brief.

The matching considers:
- Product category (sofa, table, chair, etc.)
- Style compatibility with the brief
- Dimensions relative to the room
- Price range
- Visual compatibility

## Step 4: Photorealistic Rendering

With the design brief and matched products, Vista's AI generates a photorealistic image of your redesigned room. The render shows real products placed naturally in your actual space — maintaining correct perspective, lighting, and proportions.

## Step 5: Verification

After rendering, Vista verifies that the specified products are actually visible in the output. If key items are missing, it regenerates automatically to ensure what you see matches what you can buy.

## Step 6: Chat Editing

Not satisfied? Describe changes in natural language — "make the walls lighter," "swap the sofa for something darker," "add more plants." Vista regenerates with your feedback while maintaining the overall design coherence.

## The Result

You get a photorealistic image of your redesigned room where every piece of furniture is:
- Real and in stock
- Available from a local store
- Linked directly for purchase

No imaginary products. No fictional brands. Just a realistic preview of how your room could look with furniture you can actually buy.
